## 🧩 The Business Problem

Amazon's FBA false overage claim process operated within a rigid 30/45-day date range window. This arbitrary constraint meant that a significant volume of legitimate overages went unreconciled — not because they were invalid, but because the system couldn't account for them in time.

The operational consequence: millions of dollars in reconcilable overages aged out of the system every month, and associates had no structured path to claim them. Meanwhile, the date ranges between two separate CTIs (IAR and WHL) were misaligned, creating inconsistency, confusion, and missed recovery opportunities.

The problem was identified not at leadership level, but from the ground up — a frontline insight that needed someone willing to build the business case, navigate the stakeholders, and drive it all the way to implementation.

---

## 🛠️ Approach & Framework

**1. Problem Discovery & Framing**
Identified the constraint during routine analysis of false overage SIMs. Recognised that expanding the claim window from 30/45 to 45/60 days would align date ranges and unlock unreconciled claims.

**2. Impact Quantification**
Built a data-driven business case: estimated ~$383,500/month in additional savings from expanded claim opportunities. Modelled the downstream impact on unreconciled SIM volume and claim success rates across both CTIs.

**3. Stakeholder Navigation**
Submitted through the Amazon Innovation Submission (AIS) process. Managed multiple review cycles, provided additional data sets on request, addressed pushbacks, and maintained the proposal through a complex multi-team approval chain.

**4. Cross-Functional Coordination**
Partnered across Operations, Finance, Engineering/Tool Teams, and regional leadership across India, EU, and the US to align on the change scope, implementation approach, and rollout plan.

**5. Programme Rollout**
Led end-to-end delivery across 6 operational teams. Managed change adoption, updated documentation and SOPs, and tracked post-implementation metrics.

## 💡 Key Learnings

**On ownership:** The difference between spotting a problem and solving it is ownership. No one asked me to find this. No one asked me to build the case. The willingness to pick it up and drive it — even through multiple pushbacks and stakeholder rounds — is what created the impact.

**On stakeholder management:** Large organisations have many gatekeepers. The skill is not in avoiding them, but in understanding what each stakeholder needs to feel confident in saying yes — and providing exactly that.

**On quantification:** A good idea without data is just a suggestion. A good idea with a clear, credible financial model is a business case. Learning to model impact rigorously was the key to getting this programme approved.

**On patience:** This programme touched teams across three continents and required multiple rounds of alignment. Patience — not in the passive sense, but as persistent, structured follow-through — was the non-negotiable leadership quality throughout.
